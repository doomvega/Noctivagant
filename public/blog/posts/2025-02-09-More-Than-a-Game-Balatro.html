I picked up Balatro very, very early this year on a whim. I wasn't good at it per se, but I was determined, and after a few tries, won my first ever game of Balatro. It was fascinating, and a little scary, and I found myself holding my breath more than once. As a musician I was seriously vibing with the game's soundtrack, as a game developer I was taking notes about how the game reacted to my actions, as a player I just fell head-over-heels for the entire experience. It was tough. It was, at times, brutal. Perfectly planned maneuvers would be interrupted by crafty bosses or silly oversights. But there was not a single time playing Balatro that first night that made me want to quit. Nothing that made me so angry or sad that I had to step away.

As described over on BearBlog, I've been through a lot the past month; I think all of us have. With the horrors being perpetuated in the American government and instability and uncertainty across the world, it is totally understandable to be broken and scared and needing a place to hide right now. Personally, I lost a close family member. That loss has led me down a road I never anticipated I'd be walking. It's strange, because I'm not the only one grieving, and I have so much to mourn, and every person is grieving differently. So, I turned to the newest game in my library.

I played Balatro with my father while we were waiting for everyone to come home from the hospital. I turned on Joel's (Vargskelethor) playthrough of the game.
