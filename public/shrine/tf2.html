When I was fifteen years old, I met someone through Discord. Their screen name eludes me, but they introduced me to one of my most favorite things— the Team Fortress comics. It was late at night and I was warned that the comics still weren't finished after two years of waiting, but I dove into them anyway. It was instant love. Head-over-heels kind of love. Got up the next day and downloaded the game kind of love. Reading the comics, I had genuinely forgotten about the ending and was shocked when I went to read the next comic and was kicked back to the main page of the Team Fortress site.

This band of characters was so... odd, and different, and to my Tumblr-poisoned brain they were a break from the idea of "perfection". They were weird! They were awful! They snapped people's necks and knew how to bury bodies in the most efficient ways and committed medical malpractice and... they were fucking AWESOME!

I met all sorts of people through Team Fortress and learned about myself, too. I started wearing round glasses like Medic. I briefly went by "Scout" when I figured out I was non-binary. Team Fortress was a comfort for my messy brain and it still is.

I never played multiplayer matches often but one match stands out to me very clearly— it was soon after I started playing and even though I was a newbie I was determined to be the best Medic for my team. I wound up pocketing a Demoknight who was on voice, talking to me. The guy was so kind the entire time. We had each other's backs for a while before I left. He was awesome.

Team Fortress is largely responsible for who I am today. I discovered Team Fortress in a really stressful time of my life and it left a huge impact on me.

S, if you're still out there, thank you, and I hope you're doing well.
